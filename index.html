So, you may be wondering...

 Is this a loan? What is your interest rate?

A limited royalty agreement is NOT a loan by legal definition but more like factoring where we make pre-payment on your behalf to a your supplier (in this case, the marketing agency).

With a loan you have pre-determined terms and interest rates, but with factoring both the terms and rate would go up and down as a result of the actual time spent completing the due. So you can't really adopt the idea of fixed interest rate here.

While loans can be perfect for low-risk, long-term activities, it's often a previllege kept for those with hard collaterals like real estates or heavy equippments, etc., which is hardly the case for most modern day startups and small business.

Limited royalties on the other hand, are more suitable for growth / leverage activities where risks are harder to assess. In short, this is a very entrepreneur-freidnly vehicle with both sides of the table sharing one common interest - hitting that cap ceiling ASAP.

Whille the cost of capital can be higher with limited royalties, the good news is that it's actually flexibly affordable and tolerates the risks in marketing activities quite well compared to bank loans. And also the negative effect of having outside investors too soon can be bypassed with our non-dilutive approach.

Banks and equity investors sure can be a big plus, but that's ONLY AFTER you've gotten yourself a proven track record, and that's why we're here -- to get you up and ready!

 What if my sales doesn't do well?

First of all, with solid product or service in the first place, it'd not be that hard to get more sales. Especially with our budget advancing and full-time marketers backing you up.

But in case your overall sales really underperforms, we can wait up to 2x the estimated period, or roughly 5 - 6 years for you to hit the cap.

 What if I go out of business?

While we will never ask you for personal warrants, our agreement does contain a general lien over your company's assets, meaning when things go south we'd have a liquidation preference right after the banks.

Since most startups & small business nowadays do not come with hard collaterals, our lien over your company doesn't really secure much of anything, but acts more like a reminder on legal obligations for all parties involved .

 What's the guaranteed ROI for my ads?

In terms of ROI, there have been several miconceptions even among marketers themselves!

In our case we employ mainly Gross Margin ROI as you, the client's KPI = (Sales * Gross Margin - Marketing Cost) / Marketing Cost, along with other metrics like ROAS (Revenue on Ads Spent) for marketers to base their performance off.

Why use 2 sets of KPIs? For your instance, a 10 - 20 ROAS at first glacne may look remarkable, but for clients coming from low margin industries this could actually bring about a sub 0% ROI, indicating no meaningful return or a negative cash flow basis.

Since we do care that you achieve your goal without hurting yourself, we would enforce a minimal gross ROI of 0% that you could at least enjoy getting more expossures from a SAFE HOUSE.

This is also the lowest ROI our partners can guarantee according to your marketing goals and needs. Just don't advertize for the sake of advertizing! Have the goal set before spending a cent!
